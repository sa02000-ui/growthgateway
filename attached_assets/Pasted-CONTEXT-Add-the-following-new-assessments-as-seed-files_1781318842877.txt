5. Rosenberg Self-Esteem Scale (RSES)   [state, retestIntervalDays: 90 — slow-changing growth lever]
============================================================
slug: rses | category: "Who Am I" | popular_equivalent: "Self-esteem"
scientific_reference: "Rosenberg (1965)" | cronbach_alpha: 0.88 | input_type: likert_4
Scale labels (1..4): 1=Strongly Disagree, 2=Disagree, 3=Agree, 4=Strongly Agree
scoring: '+' items score 1..4; '−' items are reverse_coded (5 minus response). Sum all 10 → range 
10–40. Higher = higher self-esteem. Flag < 25 = lower self-esteem (gentle, growth-framed).
  1. (+) I feel that I am a person of worth, at least on an equal plane with others.
  2. (+) I feel that I have a number of good qualities.
  3. (−) All in all, I am inclined to feel that I am a failure.
  4. (+) I am able to do things as well as most other people.
  5. (−) I feel I do not have much to be proud of.
  6. (+) I take a positive attitude toward myself.
  7. (+) On the whole, I am satisfied with myself.
  8. (−) I wish I could have more respect for myself.
  9. (−) I certainly feel useless at times.
  10. (−) At times I think I am no good at all.
(reverse_coded: true for items 3,5,8,9,10.)
Single trait: { key: 'SE', name: 'Self-Esteem' }.
LICENSE: widely used; acknowledge Morris Rosenberg. VERIFY terms for commercial use.

6. General Self-Efficacy Scale (GSE)   [state, retestIntervalDays: 90 — growth lever]
============================================================
slug: gse-10 | category: "Who Am I" | popular_equivalent: "Confidence / self-efficacy"
scientific_reference: "Schwarzer & Jerusalem (1995)" | cronbach_alpha: 0.86 | input_type: likert_4
Scale labels (1..4): 1=Not at all true, 2=Hardly true, 3=Moderately true, 4=Exactly true
scoring: summation (range 10–40), no reverse items. Higher = greater self-efficacy.
  1. I can always manage to solve difficult problems if I try hard enough.
  2. If someone opposes me, I can find the means and ways to get what I want.
  3. It is easy for me to stick to my aims and accomplish my goals.
  4. I am confident that I could deal efficiently with unexpected events.
  5. Thanks to my resourcefulness, I know how to handle unforeseen situations.
  6. I can solve most problems if I invest the necessary effort.
  7. I can remain calm when facing difficulties because I can rely on my coping abilities.
  8. When I am confronted with a problem, I can usually find several solutions.
  9. If I am in trouble, I can usually think of a solution.
  10. I can usually handle whatever comes my way.
Single trait: { key: 'SEF', name: 'Self-Efficacy' }.
LICENSE: free with citation (Schwarzer & Jerusalem); VERIFY for commercial use.
